My Journey Through Jules Verne's
20,000 Leagues Under The Sea

Chapter One: A Runaway Reef

July 20, 1866

The British steamer Governor Higginson encounters an unidentified object below the water five miles off the eastern shores of Australia. It was thought to be a reef, then an aquatic mammal.

July 23, 1866

The British ship Christopher Columbus spots the mysterious cetacean 700 nautical leagues away from the Governor Higginson.

August 7, 1866

The French Helvetia and the British Shannon sights the cetacean, now labeled as "monster", in latitude 42° 15' and longitude -60° 35', 2,000 leagues away from the previous sighting.

Late 1866 - early 1867

There were several more sightings of the unidentified object, which sparked debates within the scientific community and the public as to what it was.

March 5, 1867

The Canadian ship Moravian was violently struck by an unmarked rock in latitude 27° 30' and longitude -72° 15'.

April 13, 1867

Three weeks after the Moravian, the Cunard Co. ship Scotia was struck in latitude 15° 12' and longitude -45° 37'. Due to the shipping company's reputation, this accident caused a public uproar against the fearsome cetacean.

Chapter Two: The Pros and Cons

June 1867

The threat of the creature, now assumed to be a kind of narwhal, was far too great to be ignored. The United States navy launched an expedition to chase down this beast with a high-speed frigate, the Abraham Lincoln. Professor Pierre Aronnax was invited to join the expedition due to his research and knowledge on marine biology.

Chapter Three: As Master Wishes

June 3, 1867

The Abraham Lincoln leaves the harbours of New York City and enters the dark waters of the Atlantic. It was headed to the Pacific Ocean, where the beast was last sighted.

Chapter Four: Ned Land

June 25, 1867

Aronnax writes that the Abraham Lincoln was 30 miles off the coast of Patagonia, only 700 miles away from the Strait of Magellan.

Chapter Five: At Random!

June 30, 1867

The Abraham Lincoln came contact with American whalers off the coast of the Falkland Islands.

July 3-7, 1867

The frigate goes around the tip of the South American continent and enters the Pacific Ocean.

July 20, 1867

The frigate crosses the Tropic of Cancer at longitude 105°.

July 27, 1867

The frigate crosses the equator in latitude 110°

August - October 1867

The Abraham Lincoln hunted for the narwhal in the northerly seas of the Pacific

November 5, 1867

The elusive beast was finally spotted by Ned Land. The frigate lay in latitude 31° 15' and longitude 136° 42', less than 200 miles away from Japan.
